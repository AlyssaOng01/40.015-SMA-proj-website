Abstract and Motivation

Abstract:

The simulation is able to reflect different scenarios based on the user's selection of different input variables.

The user has different values depending on which variable they are allowed to change, such as switching the rate of inflation from value A to value B.

Additional, the user needs to manage the TNR efforts through decisions on how to manage its queues, inventory, type of delivery and amount of workers in the stations. Most of the factors are attached to a cost, so optimizing their numbers within the given scenario is key in reducing both costs and mega-zombies.

Singapore has a growing issue is its number of cats. Furthermore, if the policy to ban cats living in HDB is lifted, there will be an exponential surge in the amount of cats living in Singapore. Potential rising inflation rates could then hike up the cost of keeping a cat and thus force owners to abandonment them.

Finally, strays cats which are not neutered tend to breed excessively which leads to the proliferation public health hazards such as bacteria growth.

Motivation:

We hope to simulate the effects of pet abandonment and the effectiveness of TNR(Trap-Neuter-Release), to raise awareness on the effects of animal abandonment and abuse.
